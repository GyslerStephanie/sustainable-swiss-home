A desktop tool for people buying or improving a home in Switzerland. Find a property, redraw and upgrade it to Minergie-grade standards, and watch the price, energy rating, and financing update with every choice — then hand the finished vision to the banks, architects, and builders who can make it real.

Product: Sustainable Swiss Home — the daydream-to-dossier tool for sustainable, Minergie-grade renovation & first-time buying in Switzerland.
01

Overview & vision

Sustainable Swiss Home turns the daydream of "what could this place become?" into a concrete, costed, financeable plan — without needing an architect in the room to start.

Today, the gap between wanting a sustainable home and having one is mostly imagination and arithmetic. A first-time buyer sees a tired 1970s apartment and can't picture the triple-glazed, heat-pumped, light-filled version it could be — let alone what that costs, what the state pays back, or who to call. Sustainable Swiss Home closes that gap on a single screen.

The product is built around one loop: choose a change → see its full impact → keep going. Every material swap, every wall moved, every heat pump added updates a live ledger of cost, energy rating, CO₂, eligible subsidies, value uplift, and monthly mortgage delta. When the vision feels right, it becomes a structured dossier that real professionals can pick up and run with.

★ The magic moment

You drag a heat pump into your basement and a slider moves: your energy label climbs from E to B, CHF 14'000 in cantonal subsidy appears, and your monthly payment ticks up by the price of a few dinners out. Suddenly the abstract becomes obvious, affordable, and yours to build.

02

The problem we solve

For the buyer / renovator

Sustainable upgrades feel expensive, complicated, and opaque. People can't visualize the outcome, can't estimate the cost, don't know which of the dozens of Swiss subsidy schemes apply, and have no idea who to hire first. So they default to doing nothing — or a cheap, non-green patch-up.

For the ecosystem

Banks, architects, and builders meet clients who arrive with vague wishes and no documentation, forcing slow, expensive discovery cycles. A buyer who shows up with a costed, energy-modelled, subsidy-aware brief is a dramatically better lead.

Our bet

If we make the financial and carbon consequences of a renovation visible and playful — and turn the result into a real handoff document — people will invest in sustainable housing far more often, and far more confidently.

04

Design principles

Five commitments that should resolve every design argument we'll have.

Numbers you can trust, in real time

Every choice updates cost, energy, and financing instantly and transparently. We always show our assumptions; we never hide behind a "request a quote" wall.

Sustainability is a lens, not a lecture

Eco impact sits shoulder-to-shoulder with cost and beauty — never preaching, always quantified. Green is the smart default, shown through outcomes, not slogans.

From daydream to dossier

Play is the entry point; a buildable plan is the exit. Nothing the user does is throwaway — every interaction quietly assembles a real document.

Swiss precision, premium calm

Dark, quiet, data-forward. The interface should feel like a precision instrument — closer to an energy certificate than a real-estate listing app.

The plan is a handoff

The product's job ends by connecting the user to humans who build. We optimize for producing the cleanest possible brief for a bank, architect, or contractor.

05

The experience, in three acts

A single narrative arc. Act II — Reimagine — is the heart, and where most design energy goes. Acts I and III are deliberately lighter rails that feed into and out of it.

Act I · Discover

Find the place

Enter a zipcode (e.g. 8003). Browse a map of plausible Zürich listings with price, size, year, and current energy rating. Pick one to reimagine — or start from "my own home."

Lightweight rail
Act II · Reimagine

Make it yours

Open the floor plan. Move walls, swap finishes, add Minergie-grade systems. A live ledger shows cost, energy rating, CO₂, subsidies, and mortgage impact reacting to every move.

★ The heart
Act III · Connect

Make it real

Your vision becomes a dossier. Get matched with banks for financing, architects for design, and builders for delivery — each one able to read the exact plan you built.

The payoff
Flow at a glance

Zipcode → Listing → Floor plan → Configure (cost · energy · finance live) → Plan summary → Dossier → Matched professionals. A user can stop after Act II with a saved vision, or push through to a real conversation in Act III.

07

Money: financing & subsidies

The differentiator you named: making financial impact legible, and turning it into a route to actually buying or building. Three engines feed the ledger.

1 · Mortgage & affordability estimate

A simplified model of Swiss residential lending, transparent about its assumptions.

Down payment
Min. 20% of purchase price; at least 10% from "hard" equity (not pension capital).
Affordability rule
Imputed costs (≈5% interest + maintenance + amortization) should stay under one-third of gross income.
Renovation financing
Value-adding green upgrades can often be folded into the mortgage; we model the new payment.
Green mortgage rates
Several Swiss banks offer rate discounts for high energy ratings — we surface these as a lever.

2 · Renovation cost breakdown

The sum of selected upgrades, grouped by trade (envelope, systems, finishes, soft costs), with low/expected/high ranges so users see uncertainty honestly rather than a false-precision single figure.

3 · Subsidies & cantonal grants

The sustainability layer

Balanced framing: always present, never preachy. We lean on Switzerland's own established yardsticks rather than inventing our own green score.

GEAK energy rating — the spine

We use the GEAK scale (the cantonal building energy certificate, A best → G worst) as the headline metric. Every plan shows its before and after rating, the way an appliance shows its energy label.

09

From vision to build

The other half of your differentiator — making a vision manageable. The configurator's output isn't a pretty picture; it's a working brief, and a set of warm introductions.

The dossier

One generated document per plan, structured so a professional can act on it immediately: the property, the before/after floor plan, the upgrade schedule, the cost breakdown with ranges, the energy model, the applicable subsidies, and the financing assumptions. Exportable as PDF and shareable by link.

The matchmaking

From the dossier, the user is routed to the right humans for their stage — each one receiving the dossier as context.

→ Banks & financing

Mortgage advisors and green-mortgage products matched to the user's affordability profile and target rating.

→ Architects & designers

Local practices filtered by Minergie experience and the scope of the plan.

→ Builders & trades

Vetted contractors and certified installers for heat pumps, PV, and envelope work.

→ Energy advisors

GEAK-certified experts to formalize the audit and unlock the advisory subsidies.

The "manageable process" promise

The dossier doubles as a phased roadmap: what to do first, what it unlocks, which subsidy deadlines matter, and which professional to engage at each step. The vision arrives at the builder already sequenced.

Scope & the prototype

What we build together next, and what waits. The prototype proves the loop — the Reimagine configurator with a live ledger — using realistic mock Zürich data.

P1Prototype
  • Lightweight Discover: zipcode entry, a small map with 4–6 mock Zürich listings, pick one.
  • The full Reimagine workspace: editable floor plan, upgrade catalog, and the live impact ledger — the heart.
  • Live cost + energy + finance recalculating on every change, with visible assumptions.
  • Plan summary + dossier preview and a static "connect" directory.
★ Build this first
P2Depth
  • Real subsidy rules engine (canton-aware) and refined cost data.
  • Richer floor-plan editing: add/move rooms, structural hints.
  • Accounts, saved projects, side-by-side plan comparison.
P3Ecosystem
  • Live professional marketplace with real partners & lead handoff.
  • Bank integrations for genuine pre-qualification.
  • Expansion beyond Zürich to all Swiss cantons.
12

Open questions

Decisions worth making before — or as — we build the prototype.

  • How much floor-plan editing is "enough" for the prototype to feel magical without becoming a CAD tool?Suggest: select + swap + place systems first; defer wall-moving to P2.

  • Do we start from a real listing or from "my own home" — which entry point do we lead with?

  • How precise must the live numbers be to feel trustworthy yet not falsely exact? Where do we show ranges vs. single figures?

  • Is the dossier the product's true output (a lead-gen brief), or is the in-app exploration the point?This shapes the whole business model.

  • Do we want a short product handle for nav & URLs (e.g. SSH / swisshome), or always spell out the full name?Affects logo lockup, favicon, and the in-app header.

  • Which canton(s) for v1 data — Zürich only, or Zürich + a contrast canton to prove the subsidy engine generalizes?
